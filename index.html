Rex Orange County

Alexander James O'Connor, known professionally as Rex Orange County, is an English singer, songwriter and multi-instrumentalist.

"As long as you're being yourself and putting out what you want to put out, it'll be the best thing you could do."

About Me

Blending a way with words and elements of jazz, hip-hop, soul, and bedroom electronica into a sophisticated type of singer/songwriter fare, Rex Orange County is the performance alias of English musician Alex O'Connor. Raised in the village of Grayshott along the border of Hampshire and Surrey, O'Connor studied music at the BRIT School in London as a teenager. Gaining entrance on his main instrument, drums, he also sang and played guitar, and picked up piano and production software skills while working on his first album as a student there. With the "O.C." derived from O'Connor, he used the Rex Orange County moniker to self-release bcos u will never be free for streaming as a 17-year-old in 2015.

"I'm not very good at a lot of things that aren't music. This is the only thing that I love doing, so, like, why wouldn't I make this my whole life?"

Life Story

Alexander James O'Connor was born on 4 May 1998, in Grayshott, England in Grayshott, England, grew up in a musical household. His mother was a music teacher, which helped foster his love for music from an early age. At the age of 5, he began playing the drums, and by his teenage years, he was learning piano and guitar, as well as singing in choirs. These diverse musical experiences laid the foundation for his unique sound, blending jazz, hip-hop, and indie-pop. He later attended the BRIT School, a renowned performing arts school in London, where his musical skills blossomed further.

Rex Orange County released his debut album bcos u will never b free in 2015, while still a student at the BRIT School. The album caught the attention of Tyler, The Creator, who invited him to contribute to his 2017 album Flower Boy. This collaboration helped Rex gain international recognition, especially through his standout performance on the song "Foreword." Building on this momentum, he released his second album, Apricot Princess, in 2017, which received critical acclaim for its heartfelt lyrics and genre-blending style.

In 2019, Rex Orange County released Pony, his most successful album to date, which debuted at number three on the UK Albums Chart. The album's themes of mental health, love, and self-doubt resonated with listeners, marking him as a voice for a generation navigating emotional challenges. In 2022, he released another album, Who Cares?, which returned to themes of personal growth and vulnerability. As of 2024, O'Connor released hist latest album, The Alexander Technique.

"I want to make myself better and get working with some of my biggest influences and make the best music I can."

Careers

O'Connor debut album, bcos u will never b free, was self-released in 2015 while he was still a student at the BRIT School. This lo-fi project, made in his bedroom, blended elements of jazz, indie pop, and soul, capturing attention with its raw, unpolished sound. Despite its homemade production, the album introduced listeners to O'Connor distinctive voice and songwriting, full of introspective lyrics and quirky melodies. It gained significant attention on SoundCloud, leading to the eventual discovery by Tyler, The Creator, which would mark a turning point in his career.

In 2017, O'Connor released Apricot Princess, his second album, which marked a more polished and mature step in his discography. The album was a tribute to his then-girlfriend, Thea, and explored themes of love, happiness, and vulnerability. Musically, Apricot Princess incorporated a wider array of instrumentation, featuring lush orchestration alongside O'Connor signature heartfelt lyrics. The album received widespread critical acclaim for its honesty and innovation, further solidifying O'Connor place in the indie music scene. Songs like "Loving is Easy" and "Television/So Far So Good" became fan favorites, enhancing his growing reputation.

Pony, released in 2019, was O'Connor first major-label album under RCA Records. The album took a more introspective turn, delving into themes of anxiety, self-doubt, and mental health, set against a backdrop of vibrant pop melodies and orchestral flourishes. Tracks like "10/10" and "Pluto Projector" showcased his vulnerability, resonating with a broad audience and earning both commercial and critical success. Pony debuted at number three on the UK Albums Chart and entered the top 10 on the US Billboard 200, making it his most successful release to date and cementing his reputation as an emotionally raw and innovative artist.

"I like being at the front - it's where I'm meant to be."

Rex Orange County
